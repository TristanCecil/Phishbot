Business & Account Management

What is RevenueHits?
RevenueHits is an innovative Performance Ad Network that helps publishers to generate more revenues, using a state-of-the-art contextual & geo-targeted Ad-Serving technology. RevenueHits assist publishers to monetize various online assets, including websites, add-ons, mobile sites, widgets, toolbars and many more. RevenueHits is owned and operated by Intango Ltd.- a privately-held startup company founded in 2008 by a team of experienced tech professionals, finance experts and successful online marketers.
Why should I join RevenueHits as a Publisher?
With more than 2 billion impressions a day, RevenueHits promises 100% fill on ALL GEOS, based on a huge pool of offers from a variety of advertisers and networks worldwide.
What eCPM can I make with RevenueHits?
Some publishers enjoy a ratio of up to 30$ eCPM while others make only few cents with their traffic. eCPM depends on many factors uncontrolled by RevenueHits, such as ads positioning, traffic geo, users' behaviour, site content etc. With a decent amount of traffic, RevenueHits will provide you maximum-efficiency within few days, including all remnant traffic up to 100% fulfilment of traffic potential.
What technical expertises are needed to become a RevenueHits publisher?
No special technical expertises are needed to become a RevenueHits publisher.
How much does it cost to use the RevenueHits platform?
Absolutely nothing! Sign up and start earning without a single fee.
What offers are promoted by RevenueHits?
Most RevenueHits offers are performance based. There are also lead generations offers as well as CPA deals.
Does RevenueHits have any in-house offers?
In parallel to the professional Ad-Network services, RevenueHits maintains its own research and development unit, which is in charge of several in-house web-assets, aiming for best user engagement possible. These get to participate in the RevenueHits 'game' in forms of attractive offers, which their performance-success is shared with you - the publisher.
What restrictions should I follow when working with RevenueHits?
RevenueHits aims to maintain a decent, respectable business. RevenueHits publishers should avoid any of the following: (i) Place tags that haven't been specifically approved for participation in the RevenueHits.com Network on any Publisher Website. Please note that RevenueHits.com may approve additional Publisher Pages or Publisher Websites in writing (email being sufficient) in its sole discretion. (ii) Alter, reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, sell, rent, lease, sublicense, transfer, or otherwise make available the Network Tags, Creatives or any copies of them to any third party, or create derivative works from them. (iii) Copy the Network Tags or Creatives for a purpose other than the performance of this Agreement, for backup, archival or disaster recovery.

In addition, RevenueHits publishers shall not: (iv) Place Network Tags on blank web pages or on web pages with no content; clutter Creatives (e.g. place banners on top of one another, so that more than 2 ads are next to each other), or place Creatives in a fashion that may be deceptive to the visitor in that it causes the Creatives to look like editorial content. (v) Serve Creatives from any location other than the RevenueHits.com server. Images loaded from a different location will not count towards any statistic or payment.

Lastly, RevenueHits publishers' must avoid the following tactics: (vi) Induce persons to click on Creatives based on incentives (e.g. rewards programs). (vii) Use unsolicited email to promote Creatives. (viii) Use auto-spawning browsers, or automatic redirection of visitors, blind text links, misleading links, forced clicks (i.e. requiring a visitor to click on a Creative because their browser has been hijacked, or because a Creative has been altered so that it's impossible to close without clicking it); or any other method that may lead to artificially high numbers of delivered clicks.

Technical & Troubleshooting

What tag-formats does RevenueHits use?
RevenueHits uses JavaScript tag formats that seamlessly integrates into any site or app. If for any reason, a different format is needed, please contact us with details about your needs.
What ad-formats does RevenueHits support?
RevenueHits supports all common banner dimensions, as well as Pops & Interstitial formats.
I can't log into RevenueHits. What should I do?
First, note that passwords are case-sensitive. Second, try re-setting your password following the 'Reset Password' on RevenueHits login page. If resetting the password doesn't help, please contact RevenueHits using the Contact-Us form.
How come my stats shows many impressions but only few clicks?
Low CTR usually indicates on 'visibility' issues. If these are banners, make sure tags are clearly visible and well-positioned, preferably above the fold/scroll. With any ad format, make sure tags do not overlap each other, hidden behind other page elements or conflict with other advertisement entities.

Note that CTR of %1.00 is considered average in most scenarios. If traffic volume has not yet reached 1000 impressions a day, try increasing traffic volumes on relevant placements. RevenueHits studies the nature of your traffic by testing various ads on your placements, to come up with the right, converting mix for your visitors. This 'learning period' requires sufficient traffic volume per placement per day to run efficiently, so make sure adequate traffic is sent daily and keep your eyes on the records.

Finance, Payments & Revenue

How does RevenueHits pay?
RevenueHits pays in net 30 terms. For example, January earnings will be paid not later than end of February. 'Earnings' per month are your revenues for the current period. The accumulated 'Balance' will be paid in the following month.
What payment methods does RevenueHits support?
RevenueHits pays via Wire, PayPal or Payoneer. Please make sure your payment details are accurate at all time, under 'Billing information' in the 'Account Info' section.
How can I set or change my preferred payment method?
You can switch between available payment methods any time, during the sign-up process or under 'Billing information' in the 'Account Info' section, once logged in. Please note that a payment-method change performed after the 15th will be processed on the following month (i.e. a payment method change on the 18th will impact only next month payment).
How frequently stats are updated in publisher dashboard?
Your revenues are calculated once a day and become available until 12pm CST. Although revenues are updated daily, they are still subject to a monthly audit before becoming final. Since stats are gathered from different advertisers and agencies, please allow up to 2 days for stats-update to complete.
My earning shows $1,000. Will I be paid for this exact amount?
Payments are according to the records available in RevenueHits dashboard. RevenueHits absorbs its own bank fees. However, please note that the payee bank or the correspondent bank may charge additional fees for processing the payment.
My stats shows impressions & clicks but no revenues. Will I get paid for this?
RevenueHits is a performance based network. It therefore pays a revenue-share out of the publisher's traffic sales. RevenueHits does not pay for impressions or clicks.
What's RevenueHits policy with regards to Taxes?
RevenueHits.com assumes no responsibility for paying income taxes on behalf of its Publishers. By participating in the RevenueHits.com Network, Publisher assumes complete and sole responsibility for any taxes owed as a consequence of such participation and agrees to indemnify and hold RevenueHits.com harmless from any such taxes.
My payment details have changed post payment. What can be done?
To ensure timely payment, It is the Publisher obligation to update his/her account and/or payment details with RevenueHits.com, so these will reflect the most up-to-date information. In case a payment was made with out-dated account details, please update RevenueHits using the online form. Please note that the publisher will be charged for the extra fees derived from payment roll-back and re-issue, in case such charges will follow.
