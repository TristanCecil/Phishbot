Va. educators use project-based learning to engage students
5/23/2016 ⋅ Charlottesville Tomorrow (Va.)

Teachers in a Virginia high school have been working to reach more ninth-graders in a project-based learning program. As part of the pilot program, core subject teachers spend the first half of the day team-teaching subjects through hands-on, real-world projects that educators say have engaged even those students who had been quiet in class.

Finance
"Brexit" would inflict yearlong recession, Osborne says
5/23/2016 ⋅ Bloomberg⋅The Wall Street Journal (tiered subscription model)⋅Financial Times (tiered subscription model)

A UK Treasury report says the country's withdrawal from the EU would cause a yearlong recession. Under the "shock scenario," 2018 GDP would increase by 0.7%, instead of an existing forecast of 4.3%; inflation would rise sharply; and house prices would stall. Under the "severe shock" scenario, house prices would decline about 10% and GDP would be 6 percentage points lower. "With exactly one month to go to the referendum, the British people must ask themselves this question: Can we knowingly vote for a recession?" Chancellor George Osborne said.

Food & Beverage
Whole Foods looks to expand shopper base with 365 stores
5/23/2016 ⋅ Bloomberg

Whole Foods Market will open its first 365 concept store in Los Angeles this week, featuring private-label items at lower price points, more prepared foods and about a third of the selection found at its traditional stores. The 365 strategy is part of an effort to draw new shoppers who are outside of Whole Foods' usual demographics, including young consumers and those who have less of a disposable income.

Health Care
CDC report examines cleanliness of public pools
5/20/2016 ⋅ Los Angeles Times (tiered subscription model)⋅HealthDay News

CDC researchers found that nearly 80% of public aquatic venues had at least one safety violation, with 1 in 5 kiddie and wading pools closed after failing safety inspections. The findings in the CDC's Morbidity and Mortality Weekly Report, based on 2013 data involving 84,187 routine inspections of 48,632 public water play facilities in Arizona, California, Florida, New York and Texas, also showed that 15% of the pools had improper pH levels, while 13% had inadequate safety equipment and 12% had insufficient disinfectant levels.

Samsung finds success in breaking from old ways
5/23/2016 ⋅ Reuters

Samsung Electronics has managed to stabilize its sagging smartphone business through a series of steps that change the way the South Korean firm has done business in the past, Reuters reports. Infrastructure
Ga., S.C. propose $5B joint port
5/23/2016 ⋅ The Atlanta Journal-Constitution (free content)

Jasper Ocean Terminal, a $5 billion proposed joint port for South Carolina and Georgia, is raising controversy. Some are concerned the new port could mean less business at the Port of Savannah and intensify competition between the two states. However, "[i]t'll put thousands of people to work and add billions of dollars of tax revenue," says Jimmy Allgood of the Georgia Ports Authority. "It could really be a huge engine driving the South Carolina and Georgia economies."

Energy & Chemicals
Editorial: Mass. needs offshore wind, hydro to meet 2020 goals
5/23/2016 ⋅ The Boston Globe (tiered subscription model)

Massachusetts must embrace both offshore wind development and hydro sources if it wants to curb its carbon emissions 25% by 2020, writes the editorial board of The Boston Globe. Rather than pitting offshore wind and hydro against each other, lawmakers should advance legislation that opens the door for both, argues the board. "The fact of the matter is that right now only hydro can meet the state's goals on the state's timeline. But the state will need wind, too, so farsighted legislation would provide for both," it writes.
